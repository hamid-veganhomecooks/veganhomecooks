seitan recipe for @skrimem (for ham specifically - omit tomato paste, dark soy sauce, maple syrup, and paprika for more of a turkey, in which case i’d add poultry spice)

  • 1 block extra firm tofu, drained (i buy 350g packs; use whatever’s in there instead of worrying about weighing it outside of the package)
  • 2Tbsp tapioca starch (or 1T cornstarch)
  • 30g nutritional yeast
  • 1/2Tbsp garlic powder
  • 1/2Tbsp onion powder
  • 2tsp smoked paprika
  • 1tsp liquid smoke
  • 1/2tsp baking powder
  • 2tsp msg (optional)
  • 1tsp dark soy sauce
  • 1tsp tomato paste
  • 1tsp maple syrup
  • 1Tbsp bouillon (i use veg beef bouillon, swap to regular veg or veg chicken for turkey slices)
  • 2Tbsp olive oil
  • couple splashes of water (see instructions)
  • APPROX 180g vital wheat gluten (see instructions)
  1. add all ingredients except for the vital wheat gluten water into a large food processor, and process until smooth
  2. add water, a tablespoon at a time, until the mixture reaches a hummus-like consistency
  3. add the vital wheat gluten and process on high. you may want to remove half the mixture first before adding the vwg if your food processor is on the smaller side. you may need a little more or a little less vwg - horrifically, i eyeball this so my measurement is a guess, but your final “dough” should be firm, uniform, and not dry
  4. process until the the consistency goes from small crumbly bits to a more uniform dough
  5. turn out onto a clean counter and knead a couple of times to work out any bumpy bits.
  6. shape the dough into two logs with your hands
  7. place onto a piece of parchment paper large enough to wrap around the entire log. if you’d like, you can add a little more oil here and add a rub - for ham i’d recommend dijon and maple syrup. for turkey i like to add dried sage and/or thyme with black pepper. distribute the rub well, wrap in the paper, and finally wrap tightly with foil.
  8. preheat the oven to 350. allow the dough to rest while the oven preheats
  9. bake for 1hr, flipping after 30 min 10.allow to cool for at least 30 min without unwrapping. then, unwrap and allow to cool to room temperature before storing in an airtight container
  10. the next day, after a night in the fridge, slice or cut into the shapes you want

btw - this (for me) makes 2-3 logs of seitan depending on how much you would like of each variety, if you decide to do more than one. even if you want it all the same flavour i’d still recommend breaking it into two equally-sized logs - 1hr in the oven will be enough for 2 or 3 logs, but not enough for 1
