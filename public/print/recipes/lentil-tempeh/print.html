Ingredients

  • 2 cups dry brown lentils
  • 1.7-ish liters water (for boiling)
  • 2 tbsp white vinegar
  • 2.5 tsp tempeh starter (Raprima bulk starter — store in the fridge)

Equipment

  • 2 sandwich bags
  • Toothpick (for poking holes in bags)
  • Small kitchen scale
  • Oven with a functioning light (acts as incubator)
  • Large mixing bowl
  • Kitchen towel (for cooling lentils)
  • Strainer
  • Racks
  • Thermometer

Instructions

Step 1 — Cook the lentils

Rinse and cook lentils for 12-13 minutes but NO MORE. Meanwhile poke lots of holes in the sandwich bags w the toothpick.

Step 2 — Cool the lentils

Drain and let cool preferably spread out on a kitchen towel laid over a large rack. Come back in 30min or so

Step 3 — Mix in starter

Put lentils in a large mixing bowl with vinegar and tempeh starter and mix really well. I put on a mask during this step

Step 4 — Fill the bags

Put lentils into the bags using the kitchen scale to ensure you’ve got about the same amount in each (usually about 16oz). After you close the bags, gently even them out and press a bit flat.

Step 5 — Set up your incubator

Place on a rack in the oven and turn on the oven light but use other pots to keep your tempeh out of direct oven light because otherwise it will get toooo hot. This will act as your incubator.

Step 6 — Ferment for 2 days

Let it grow for 2 days! For the first day leave it alone but after that you will see growth (I took a reference picture, see the moisture and “mold” forming) and this is when you monitor the temp w a thermometer, don’t let it go above 95F for prolonged periods. In the last 18 hours or so, you can take it out of the oven esp if it’s warm at 95F and what I like to do is run a desk fan at low setting and let it air and cool and this I’ve seen to bring it down to 88-92F.

Step 7 — Cook and enjoy

Once the tempeh is thick and ready like in the pictures, take it out of the plastic, cut it up, pan sear, splash some soy sauce near the end and thats all you need to enjoy this tasty juicy lentil tempeh.
