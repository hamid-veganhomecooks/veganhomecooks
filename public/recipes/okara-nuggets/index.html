Ingredients

  • Wet okara (cooked okara works well, but raw is fine too since these get cooked a lot — if your okara is dry, add any liquid like plant milk, broth, tomato product, etc.)
  • Vital wheat gluten (VWG)
  • Flour
  • Chick’n bouillon (or any bouillon, or whatever spices you want)

Instructions

Make the dough

Use a food processor to blend for 30 seconds or until well combined. The dough should clump up and come away from the walls of the food processor, like seitan.

Let it autolyse in the food processor for anywhere from 15 minutes to several hours.

After that, you can optionally blend in some dense or frozen tofu, beans, or veggies. Don’t blend too much — these can be chunky.

First bake

Squish the dough into nugget shapes and lay them on a baking sheet. Bake for 15 minutes each side at 380°F. After this they’re edible but squishy.

Second cook (pick one)

  • Marinade + air fry — make a marinade and air fry until crispy
  • Pan fry — cut them up and pan fry
  • Panko crust + bake — make a wet dredge with plant milk and flax (optionally with butter or oil) and a dry dredge with panko, starch or flour, and any spices. Wet dredge then dry dredge the nuggets, optionally spray with oil (without oil the panko will stay white and may burn quickly, so keep an eye on them). Bake at 400°F or broil until golden on each side.
