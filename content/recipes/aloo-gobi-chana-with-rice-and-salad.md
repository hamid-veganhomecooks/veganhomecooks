# My Lazy(ish) Chickpea & Potato Curry 

Let’s get one thing straight: **I don’t really cook with recipes.** 

I write this down more as a *general process* than a strict set of rules, because when I made this the other night, I was busy and needed it on the table fast. I don't care about exact measurements—I go entirely by **vibes**. 

> **Pro-tip:** If I actually have extra time on my hands, I’ll toast whole spices and grind them fresh. It does taste better. But on a Tuesday night? Store-bought curry powder is a hero.

---

## 🍚 The Rice Base
I toss **2 cups of basmati** (or whatever you have) into the rice cooker with a few **cardamom pods** and **2 cups of water**, then hit "start." That’s my timer for the rest of the meal.

---

## 🥔 The Veggies
While the rice cooks, I steam **2 diced potatoes** and a whole **bag of frozen cauliflower**. 

- I use a pot with a steaming insert until the potatoes are fork-tender.  
- But honestly? The **microwave** works perfectly fine here too. *(Don't tell a chef I said that.)*

---

## 🍛 The Gravy (The Only Part That Needs a Stove)
In a separate pot, I sauté some **onions**, **ginger-garlic paste**, and a squeeze of **tomato paste**. 

Cook it until the tomato paste darkens and turns a bit sweet—this takes about **3–4 minutes** and builds real flavor fast. 

Then I:
1. Add a few heavy shakes of **pre-mixed curry powder** and a splash of **water** to turn it into a gravy.  
2. Toss in your steamed potatoes, cauliflower, and a **can of chickpeas**.  
3. Stir everything until it’s beautifully coated.  

At the very end, I sprinkle in some **pre-mixed garam masala**. 

> **Why late?** Adding it at the end keeps that sweet, aromatic punch from burning off. 

---

## 👩‍🍳 The "Chef" Debate
I know the pros will say you need to chop everything by hand and layer your spices in a specific order. And look, if you're hosting a dinner party, go for it. But for a weeknight meal? **The flavor difference is *marginal*.** 

If you’re really short on time, just throw a whole onion in a food processor, blend it smooth, and cook it all at once with the ginger and tomato paste. Comes out totally fine.

---

## 🥗 The Fresh Side
To cut through the richness, I make a quick salad:

- A few leaves of **romaine**  
- Some **sprouts** from my sprouting tray  
- Grated **carrot**  
- A splash of **red wine vinegar**  

Top the whole bowl with fresh **cilantro** (coriander leaves) for that pop of green.

---

## ⏱️ The Time-Saver Secret
This whole meal only takes as long as your rice cooker needs. 

For years, I only had a **single burner**, so I’d:
- Microwave the veggies  
- Use the rice cooker  
- Only dirty **one pot** for the gravy  

> **Want to make it richer?** Stir in a splash of **plant milk** (coconut or oat work great) right at the end. It gives it a creamy, luxurious feel without any extra work.

---

## 📋 Quick Vibe Check (Troubleshooting)
- **Too thick?** Add a splash of water or broth.  
- **Too bland?** Add more salt or a squeeze of lemon.  
- **Too spicy?** Stir in that plant milk.
