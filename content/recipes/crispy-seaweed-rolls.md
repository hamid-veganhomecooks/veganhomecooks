# Crispy Seaweed Rolls

**Source:** Korean Women's Table http://xhslink.com/o/2gtIzEBnffS (Non vegan)  
**Category:** Snack / Side Dish  
**Prep time:** 5 minutes  
**Cook time:** 5 minutes  

---

## Ingredients

- Seaweed (nori)
- Vietnamese rice paper
- White sesame seeds
- Paprika powder
- Vegan parm (hempseed and nutritional yeast)
- Cooking oil

---

## Instructions

1. Sprinkle white sesame seeds, paprika powder, and vegan parm evenly over the seaweed.
2. Soak the rice paper until soft, then place one sheet on top and one sheet underneath the seasoned seaweed.
3. Lightly spray with oil, roll everything up tightly, and cut into small bite-sized pieces.
4. Place in an air fryer or oven and bake at **180°C (356°F)** for **5 minutes**.

---

## Tips

- For maximum crispiness, be careful not to bake too long.

𑂐ꚳ㎰ꪭ Notes:
- it's really annoying to find matching size and quantities of 0 fat nori and square rice paper, even with the abundance of options at Hmart
- I used roasted white and black sesame (not hulled), gochugaru, msg, and salt, in that order from coarse to fine
- the rice paper only needs a quick dip, but requires a firm press and rub like sealing an envelope really good, or else you will get the filling everywhere in your kitchen and oven
- I brushed a little algae oil on the inside like she did with the spray, but if you want to get these crispy and golden as the video, you'll need a decent amount of oil sprayed honestly on the rings when cut
- I have 0 clue how hers puffed up and were so uniform besides the oil, it's near impossible to roll a ring with a decent air gap, unless there's a way to reduce the rice paper moisture to the perfect amount
- I recommend making all the rolls first, occasionally peeling them off your board of choice, then cutting them all at once when they go into the oven
- I did 400F for closer to 10 minutes as opposed to her ~350F for 5, but I also didn't use a high flow convection oven, just a toaster with an air fry setting and a decent blower
- if you pressed them right, the filling won't fall out even when they separate a little bit after cooking
- really tasty and filling, I think they'll last on the shelf for a bit too if I can stop eating them
